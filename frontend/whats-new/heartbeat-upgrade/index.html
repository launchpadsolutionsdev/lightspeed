Heartbeat Evolves: Dedicated Sub-Pages, Smarter Data & Real-Time Freshness

Heartbeat now splits into two focused sub-pages — a Live Dashboard for real-time raffle monitoring and a dedicated Shopify Intelligence page for your store’s daily snapshot — with city-level geographic tracking, live freshness timestamps, and a unified Shopify experience.

Since launching Heartbeat last week, we’ve been watching how teams use it during live events. The biggest piece of feedback: the page was doing too much. Real-time raffle velocity and a Shopify store snapshot are both valuable, but they serve different needs at different moments. Today’s update gives each its own space and adds several new capabilities.

Two Dedicated Sub-Pages

Heartbeat now has a collapsible sub-menu in the sidebar, just like the Response Assistant. Click the arrow to expand it and you’ll see two focused pages:

  • Live Dashboard — Everything related to your active raffle: sales velocity ticker, vital signs, surge detection, payment breakdown, package performance, hourly heatmaps, jackpot comparisons, and the live orders feed. Polls every 5 seconds for real-time data.
  • Shopify Intelligence — Your Shopify store’s daily snapshot: revenue, orders, average order value, refunds, geographic hotspots, top products, top buyers, and discount code tracking. Auto-refreshes every 2 minutes.

Each sub-page only runs the polling it needs. When you’re on the Live Dashboard, Shopify queries don’t fire. When you switch to Shopify Intelligence, the 5-second raffle ticker stops. This means less unnecessary network traffic and a snappier experience.

City-Level Geographic Tracking

The “Where Orders Are Coming From” map now shows city-level detail. You’ll see entries like “Thunder Bay, Ontario” or “Toronto, Ontario” instead of just a province-level breakdown. Lightspeed pulls address data from shipping, billing, and customer default addresses to give you the most accurate geographic picture of your buyers — especially useful for digital products and raffle tickets where shipping addresses aren’t always present.

Live Freshness Timestamps

The Shopify Intelligence header now shows exactly when data was last refreshed — “Updated 3:45 PM ET” — so you never have to wonder whether you’re looking at stale numbers. A footer confirms the refresh cadence and when data resets. If the system detects that order data hasn’t synced in over 30 minutes, a visible warning appears so your team knows to check the connection.

Always-Live Data from Shopify

Shopify Intelligence now pulls data directly from the Shopify API every time you open the page — up to 10,000 of your most recent orders, computed on the fly and cached for two minutes. No more waiting for sync cycles, no more stale pre-computed tables, no more mismatched numbers. What you see is what Shopify has, period.

Unified Shopify Experience

The standalone Shopify Analytics page that previously lived in the sidebar has been consolidated into Heartbeat › Shopify Intelligence. All your Shopify store data — revenue, orders, top products, buyer activity, geographic hotspots, and discount codes — now lives in one place alongside your raffle data. One tool, one sidebar entry, zero context-switching.

Keyboard & Command Palette

Both new sub-pages are accessible from the command palette (Cmd+K or Ctrl+K). Type “heartbeat” or “shopify” to jump directly to either page. Direct URLs also work: /heartbeat/live and /heartbeat/shopify.
